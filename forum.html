U.S. Senate Advances Digital Asset Framework — XRP Named as Key Settlement Asset
Bipartisan legislation moving through the Senate would formally recognize XRP as a commodity and establish clear guidelines for its use in cross-border settlement...
TAP TO READ ▾

A sweeping digital asset regulatory framework advanced out of the Senate Banking Committee this week with rare bipartisan support. The bill explicitly categorizes XRP as a commodity under CFTC jurisdiction and clears the way for federally chartered banks to use XRP for real-time gross settlement.

The legislation, co-sponsored by senators from both parties, comes after two years of hearings in which Ripple executives testified on the efficiency gains of blockchain-based settlement. Industry observers say this is the clearest sign yet that Washington has accepted XRP’s role in the financial infrastructure.

Ripple’s legal team called the development “a watershed moment” adding that the company expects to see accelerated adoption among U.S. financial institutions once the bill is signed into law. The House version of the bill is expected to move to the floor within 60 days.

Three Major Banks Announce Live XRP Settlement Pilots — RippleNet Expands to 65 Countries
A trio of top-ten global banks have quietly gone live with XRP-based settlement pilots, pushing RippleNet’s active corridor count to a record high...
TAP TO READ ▾

Three globally systemically important banks — two European and one Japanese — have moved from testing to live production on XRP-based payment corridors, according to sources familiar with the deployments. The institutions declined to be named publicly but the corridor data is visible on-chain and has been confirmed by multiple blockchain analytics firms.

RippleNet now spans 65 countries, up from 55 at the start of 2025. The new corridors cover high-volume remittance lanes between Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Europe — some of the most expensive and slowest in traditional correspondent banking.

Transaction costs on the new XRP corridors are reported to be 94% lower than the SWIFT alternative, with settlement occurring in under four seconds. Industry analysts say these numbers, once widely published, will be difficult for rival networks to match and could trigger a broader banking migration toward XRP over the next 18 months.
